US Immigration Programs

E-2 & E-1 Programs

Investor & Trade Work Permits

The E-2 visa is for entrepreneurs from treaty countries who make a substantial investment in a U.S. business, while the E-1 visa is for individuals or companies engaged in significant trade between their home country and the U.S.

The E-2 visa has no fixed minimum investment, but the amount must be sufficient to operate a viable business. The E-1 visa also has no investment requirement, as it is based on regular and substantial trade, which must primarily be conducted with the U.S.

The E-2 visa is indefinitely renewable as long as the business remains operational, while the E-1 visa is also renewable as long as trade activity continues.

    Key Benefits:

  • No fixed minimum investment for E-2
  • No investment required for E-1
  • Renewable as long as business or trade continues
  • Spouses eligible for work authorization
  • Flexible entry for business owners and traders
